Digestion Simulation

Written by: Nice

INTRODUCTION

There are laboratories in our Bisacool Bataan Science School where experiments and scientific research are performed. Our school's laboratories assist students in better understanding topics and applying their learning to practical situations. Students learn to utilize scientific tools and norms, which will help them grasp the nature of science. By this, it is critical to comprehend the digestive system because it plays a significant function in everyone's general health by providing nutrients to our bodies. We must be mindful that our bodies need nutrients from food to function effectively and be healthy. It is also crucial to understand how each of our organs functions in our digestive system so we know how to care for these organs in our bodies. We, students, have recently performed an activity that simulates the steps of food processing, which helps us learn how the digestive system works. It displays the breakdown of food into nutrients that the body may use for energy, cell repair, and development. It teaches the six digestive processes: ingestion, digestion, absorption, and extraction, using alternate materials to demonstrate amusingly and easily how they function.

STAGES OF FOOD PROCESSING

Crushing Stage

The stage where we put the banana and crackers in a zip lock simulates the process of ingestion where large complex molecules are ingested or eaten as the banana and crackers represent food and the zip lock represents the stomach. During the swallowing stage, we observed that we had combined the banana, crackers, and lemon juice with water. This mimicked swallowing by simulating saliva from the mouth. Salivary glands produce saliva, a digestive liquid that helps food pass more smoothly through the esophagus and into the stomach. During the swallowing and crushing stage, we noticed that we put the crackers, banana, water, and lemon juice in a zip-locked bag. This simulation mimicked the ingestion where large complex molecules are broken down into small pieces as the zip-locked bag represents the stomach. During the crushing stage, we noticed that the food became smaller. This simulates the first step of digestion which increases the surface area of food to allow a more efficient breakdown by enzymes. It also mimicked the action of the stomach. During the addition of orange juice or the digestion and absorption stage, we noticed the insides of the stomach or the zip lock bag turn into a soup-like liquid. Orange juice represents stomach acids that break down the food, kill bacteria, and the digestive enzymes split up the proteins. We think that the food within the zip lock bag includes biological substances such as proteins, carbohydrates, nucleic acids, and lipids.

Transfer Stage

The liquid pouring from the stocking demonstrates osmosis and water absorption in the small intestines. The tray depicts the intestinal lumen. The feces are represented by the stuff left in the stocking. Pushing the partially hardened material from the tights demonstrates extraction. Once the stomach contents feel like a thick soup liquid, we cut a tiny hole in one corner and slowly press into the tights or stocking (small intestine). The small intestine is made up of several thin structures known as villi. These have a vast surface area for absorbing food molecules. We hold the tights over the tray and slowly squeeze the liquid out. The liquid in the tray indicates the nutrients that the body receives and utilizes. Food left behind the tights symbolizes waste items that cannot be digested.

DOCUMENTING SIMULATION OF ABSORPTION

Simulation of Absorption

The most absorbent paper towel is the four-folded paper towel. The paper towels absorbed water since the layers generated air spaces. A small intestine is an organ in the digestive system that can be compared to a paper towel. Paper towels absorb water, and villi absorb digested food. The villi, like paper towels, have a larger surface area into which substances can be absorbed. A thicker towel, such as a four-folded paper towel, has more fibers to absorb water. Tissue and paper towel fibers are composed of cellulose molecules.

DOCUMENTING SIMULATION of BILE ACTION

Simulation of Bile Action

The citrus juice stimulates bile movement in the small intestine by breaking down the vegetable oil, which then combines with the water in cup B. It's comparable to bile in the small intestine breaking down lipids into fatty acids. The purpose of the bile is to help in the digestion of lipids in the duodenum. Bile is a fluid produced and retained in the gallbladder by the liver that aids digestion and breaks down lipids into fatty acids. Emulsification is the action of bile on fat and is an example of mechanical digestion. Since humans eat so quickly, the amylase contained in saliva digests little carbs while food is in the mouth. Bile is a mechanical digestion that contributes to the surface area to volume ratio, making enzymes more likely to break down lipids entirely.
